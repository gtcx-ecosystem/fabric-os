## CRM / account owner requirements

Before DSLC can pass, the release owner must attach:

- named account or explicit internal pilot target;
- opportunity/customer-management record;
- commercial owner;
- customer success owner;
- support escalation owner;
- pilot start/end dates;
- activation/exit criteria.

## Onboarding and support plan

Draft onboarding sequence:

1. Confirm participant eligibility and legal readiness.
2. Confirm pilot user roles and access boundaries.
3. Issue operator onboarding instructions.
4. Schedule pilot kickoff and support window.
5. Run initial operator workflow with support owner present.
6. Record friction, incidents, blockers, and success evidence.

Support path:

- Primary owner: pending commercial assignment.
- Technical owner: fabric-os / markets-os release owner.
- Escalation: release manager.
- Incident route: pending pilot environment binding.

## Renewal, expansion, churn, and exit plan

Draft pilot outcomes:

- Convert: pilot evidence reaches DSLC + SHIP `ready/100`.
- Extend: pilot evidence is materially positive but one or more Class A/S gates remain open.
- Exit: legal, commercial, operational, or product evidence cannot reach gate.

Required closeout evidence:

- pilot outcome report;
- support/friction log;
- commercial next-step decision;
- renewal/expansion or exit owner.

## Remaining approval blockers

- Offer/SKU/pricing approval.
- Billing/payment/RevOps in-scope or explicit out-of-scope approval.
- CRM/account record and owner assignment.
